Purpose

This portfolio artifact summarizes how I design enterprise-grade AI systems that are secure, governable, operable, and financially sustainable. The focus is not on novelty, but on applying proven architecture principles to AI-enabled solutions.

Design principles

AI is treated as a first-class enterprise capability, subject to the same rigor as other critical systems:

  • Centralized access and control
  • Enterprise identity and authorization
  • Explicit network boundaries and ingress controls
  • Operational observability and auditability
  • Predictable cost management

Bottom line: Powerful AI is valuable. Unmanaged AI is risk.

Reference architecture (Build 1)

Users access AI capabilities through a controlled ingress and a centralized application/API layer. The application authenticates users via Microsoft Entra ID and accesses Azure OpenAI using managed identities. Supporting planes include identity & access management, network segmentation, observability, and cost guardrails.

Key architectural decisions (ADR summary)

ADR-001 — AI access pattern

All AI access is mediated through a centralized WebApp/API layer.

ADR-002 — Identity & authentication

Entra ID for user auth; managed identity for service-to-service access to Azure OpenAI.

ADR-003 — Network & ingress controls

Controlled ingress with standard enterprise patterns; AI services accessed only by the app layer.

ADR-004 — Observability & logging policy

Centralized telemetry with privacy-aware logging and clear retention/access policies.

ADR-005 — Cost management & guardrails

Layered controls: limits, quotas, budgets/alerts, and model selection to keep spend predictable.

What this architecture enables

Enables

  • Secure, auditable AI adoption
  • Predictable cost behavior
  • Scalable onboarding of new use cases
  • Alignment with enterprise governance and risk models

Avoids

  • Direct end-user access to AI services
  • Unmanaged API keys and secret sprawl
  • “Shadow AI” without monitoring
  • Over-logging of sensitive content by default
  • Surprise spend from uncontrolled token usage
